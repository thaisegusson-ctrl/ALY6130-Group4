# Adewole Adelana Contribution: Netflix ERM Risk Analysis

## Section Focus

This section supports the group SWOT analysis for Netflix with a focus on Enterprise Risk Management. The proposed acquisition is Netflix acquiring a smaller gaming or interactive entertainment company. This type of acquisition could help Netflix expand beyond traditional movie and television streaming and strengthen its position in digital entertainment.

From an ERM perspective, the acquisition should not be viewed only as a growth opportunity. It also creates risks that can affect people, processes, technology, customer experience, information security, finance, and compliance. Netflix must identify these risks early, measure them properly, and apply controls before and after the acquisition.

---

## Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)

Key Risk Indicators are early warning signs that help Netflix know when a risk is increasing. For this acquisition, Netflix should monitor KRIs across people, process, technology, information security, finance, and customer experience.

| Risk Area | Key Risk Indicator | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| People | Percentage of employees trained on the acquired gaming or interactive platform | Low training completion can lead to poor support, user errors, and slow adoption. |
| People | Employee turnover after the acquisition | High turnover may show cultural issues, stress, or dissatisfaction after the acquisition. |
| Process | Number of delayed integration tasks | Delays may increase costs and reduce the expected value of the acquisition. |
| Process | Number of customer support tickets related to the new service | A high number of tickets may show problems with usability, access, billing, or service quality. |
| Technology | Number of system defects found during integration testing | This shows whether the acquired platform is ready to connect with Netflix’s existing systems. |
| Technology | Platform downtime per month | Frequent downtime can affect customer trust and reduce user engagement. |
| Information Security | Number of suspicious login attempts or account takeover attempts | This helps Netflix monitor cyber threats against customer accounts. |
| Information Security | Number of privacy or unauthorized data access incidents | Unauthorized access to customer data can create legal, financial, and reputational risk. |
| Financial | Actual integration cost compared with the planned budget | If costs become higher than expected, the acquisition may reduce financial value. |
| Customer Experience | Customer satisfaction score after launching the new service | This shows whether customers are accepting and enjoying the new service. |

## Technical Risk Considerations

Netflix must carefully review the technology of the company it plans to acquire. The acquired company may use different software systems, cloud services, databases, coding practices, and security controls. If these systems do not connect properly with Netflix’s current platform, customers may experience login issues, poor performance, app errors, or service disruption.

Important technical risks include:

- Difficulty connecting the acquired platform with Netflix’s existing systems
- Poor performance when many users access the platform at the same time
- Software bugs that affect customer experience
- Compatibility issues across mobile phones, smart TVs, tablets, and web browsers
- Weak backup and disaster recovery processes
- Incomplete testing before launch
- Higher-than-expected technology upgrade costs

To reduce these risks, Netflix should use a phased integration plan. The company should first test the acquired platform in a controlled environment before making it available to all customers. Netflix should also assign a technical risk owner to monitor system performance, downtime, defects, and customer complaints.

---

## Information Security Risk Considerations

Information security is one of the most important risks in this acquisition. Netflix handles customer accounts, email addresses, payment-related information, viewing history, and user preferences. If the acquired company has weak cybersecurity controls, Netflix could face data breaches, fraud, account theft, legal penalties, and reputational damage.

Important information security risks include:

- Unauthorized access to customer accounts
- Weak password or authentication controls
- Poor protection of customer data
- Privacy issues during data migration
- Cyberattacks targeting the new platform
- Third-party vendor security weaknesses
- Lack of a strong incident response plan

To control these risks, Netflix should complete cybersecurity due diligence before completing the acquisition. This should include vulnerability testing, access control review, privacy assessment, encryption review, and incident response planning. Netflix should also monitor suspicious activity after the acquisition and make sure that customer data is protected during system migration.

## Preliminary Risk Assessment and Controls

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Risk Level | Suggested Control |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Data breach or unauthorized access | Medium | High | High | Conduct cybersecurity due diligence, use encryption, strengthen access controls, and monitor suspicious activity. |
| Technology integration failure | Medium | High | High | Use phased integration, system testing, and technical risk ownership. |
| Service downtime after launch | Medium | High | High | Use stress testing, backup systems, and incident response planning. |
| Employee resistance or poor adoption | Medium | Medium | Medium | Provide role-based training and clear communication. |
| Higher-than-planned integration cost | Medium | High | High | Track actual cost against budget and require approval for major cost increases. |
| Customer complaints after launch | Medium | Medium | Medium | Monitor support tickets, app ratings, and customer satisfaction scores. |
| Privacy compliance issue | Low to Medium | High | High | Complete privacy review, legal review, and data handling assessment. |

## ERM Connection

This acquisition should be managed as an enterprise-wide risk because it affects different parts of Netflix’s business. It is not only a technology project. It also affects customers, employees, operations, finance, legal compliance, cybersecurity, and Netflix’s reputation.

From an Enterprise Risk Management perspective, Netflix should:

1. Identify the major risks before completing the acquisition.
2. Assess each risk based on likelihood and impact.
3. Assign a risk owner to each major risk area.
4. Create controls for cybersecurity, technology, finance, customer experience, and compliance risks.
5. Monitor KRIs regularly after the acquisition.
6. Report major risk issues to leadership for quick decision-making.

This ERM approach will help Netflix make a better acquisition decision and reduce the chance of problems after the acquisition.
